CCCWA COVID Charity Meal Drive

Supporting local restaurants and vulnerable residents during COVID-19

← Back to News
Announcement 2020

The Chinese Chamber of Commerce in Washington State (CCCWA) launched a community fundraising appeal at the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, calling on its members and supporters to contribute to a charity meal drive that would support two interlinked groups: Chinese-owned restaurants facing closure, and vulnerable community members who needed food assistance.

The fundraising letter (倡议书), issued by CCCWA leadership in early 2020, framed the appeal as a moment of community solidarity — an opportunity for those with the means to help, to stand alongside neighbors, colleagues, and fellow business owners who were facing serious hardship.

How the Drive Worked

Donations collected through the campaign were channeled directly into meal orders placed with participating Chinese restaurants across the Greater Seattle area. Restaurants received payment for meals prepared at or near cost, providing a critical revenue stream at a time when their doors were otherwise closed to the public.

Prepared meals were collected by volunteer drivers — many of them CCCWA members donating their time — and delivered to hospitals, assisted living facilities, community centers, and directly to households in need. The logistics were coordinated through CCCWA's established network of contacts and volunteers, with daily routes organized across Seattle, Bellevue, Redmond, and surrounding communities.

Community Response

The response from CCCWA's membership and the broader Chinese-American community was immediate and generous. Donations came from individuals, businesses, and organizations across Washington State and from the wider Chinese diaspora, reflecting the community's deep sense of mutual obligation and care.

As the campaign grew, CCCWA continued to provide updates to donors and the public, documenting the number of meals delivered, the restaurants involved, and the communities reached. These updates became an important form of accountability and also served to encourage continued giving as the pandemic stretched into the summer months.

Impact

The charity meal drive became one of CCCWA's signature programs during the pandemic, ultimately helping to sustain multiple Chinese-owned restaurants through a period that devastated the food service industry, while delivering thousands of meals to frontline workers and at-risk community members. The program demonstrated the power of organized community action and established a model for CCCWA's crisis response work going forward.
