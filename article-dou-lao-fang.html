Dou Lao Fang Restaurant: One Pot, One World

This article is part of CCCWA's "COVID Relief Restaurant Series" (抗击疫情·爱心餐厅系列), a collection of profiles highlighting Chinese restaurants in the Greater Seattle area that participated in the organization's charity meal campaign during the COVID-19 pandemic. Each restaurant in the series represents a different story of resilience, community spirit, and generosity in the face of unprecedented hardship.

Dou Lao Fang (豆捞坊), a beloved hotpot restaurant known for its rich broths and communal dining experience, joined CCCWA's Food with Love initiative at one of the most difficult moments in the restaurant industry's history. With dining rooms shuttered and foot traffic at a standstill, the restaurant's owners faced an existential choice. They chose community.

One Pot, One Story

The name of the series profile — "One Pot, One World" (一锅一世界) — captures something essential about hotpot culture: a single pot of broth, surrounded by friends and family, represents connection, warmth, and shared experience. During the pandemic, that pot became a symbol of something larger — a restaurant reaching beyond its walls to feed strangers, and in doing so, feeding a community's sense of hope.

Dou Lao Fang's kitchen was repurposed during closures to prepare charity meals at scale. Working with the CCCWA coordination network, the restaurant prepared hundreds of boxed meals that were picked up by volunteers and delivered to frontline workers — doctors, nurses, police officers, and firefighters working long shifts to protect the city.

Supporting Both Sides of the Table

The program was designed with two goals in mind: feeding essential workers who needed support, and keeping Chinese restaurants alive. By channeling donated funds into meal orders from participating restaurants, CCCWA created a model where giving back also meant sustaining local businesses that had become pillars of the Chinese-American community.

For Dou Lao Fang's owners, participation meant more than revenue. It meant being visible as a responsible, caring member of the Seattle community at a moment when Chinese-owned businesses were navigating both economic pressure and, in some cases, unfair stigma. The restaurant's commitment to the campaign became a point of pride — proof that a hotpot restaurant could nourish a city in more ways than one.

About the Food with Love Campaign

CCCWA's Food with Love (爱心午餐) campaign ran throughout the spring and summer of 2020, coordinating dozens of Chinese-owned restaurants across Greater Seattle. The initiative was funded through a community fundraising effort and recognized by local media, including KIRO 7 News, which covered the campaign in an ongoing series highlighting its reach and impact.
