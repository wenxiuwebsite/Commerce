CCCWA Foundation Unites Community Against COVID-19

Coordinating relief and support across Greater Seattle

← Back to News
Community August 20, 2020

When COVID-19 swept through Washington State in early 2020, the Chinese-American community faced unique challenges — stigma, language barriers, economic hardship, and uncertainty. The Chinese Chamber of Commerce in Washington State (CCCWA) mobilized quickly, coordinating a wide-ranging community relief effort through its foundation arm.

Working alongside local businesses, volunteers, and community partners across Greater Seattle, CCCWA helped organize and fund multiple parallel initiatives designed to address the most urgent needs of its members and the broader community.

Food with Love (爱心午餐)

One of CCCWA's most visible relief programs was "Food with Love" (爱心午餐) — a charity meal initiative that organized Chinese restaurants to prepare and deliver hundreds of free meals daily to frontline workers at local hospitals, police stations, and fire departments. The program served a dual purpose: supporting healthcare and public safety workers during an exhausting crisis, and keeping Chinese-owned restaurants operating at a time when dining rooms were closed and revenue had collapsed.

Volunteer drivers, coordinated through CCCWA's network, picked up prepared meals from participating restaurants each day and delivered them across Seattle, Bellevue, and surrounding areas. Among the participating restaurants was Chowbus, whose delivery team became a regular presence at hospital entrances, becoming emblematic of the community's spirit of care and generosity.

Fundraising and Community Outreach

CCCWA launched a fundraising campaign to sustain the Food with Love initiative and provide emergency financial support to member businesses and families in need. The campaign received support from community donors across Washington State and beyond, reflecting the deep ties within the Chinese-American business community.

At the same time, CCCWA worked to combat misinformation and provide accurate public health information to Chinese-language audiences, partnering with healthcare professionals and government agencies to produce bilingual content on COVID prevention, testing, and available resources.

Media Recognition

The scale and impact of CCCWA's COVID relief efforts drew widespread attention. KIRO 7 News covered the "Food with Love" initiative in a multi-part series, introducing Seattle viewers to the restaurants and volunteers at the heart of the program and highlighting the Chinese-American community's contribution to the city's pandemic response.

CCCWA's relief work during COVID-19 became a defining moment for the organization, demonstrating its capacity to mobilize quickly, serve a broad constituency, and forge lasting partnerships with government, media, and the broader Seattle business community.
