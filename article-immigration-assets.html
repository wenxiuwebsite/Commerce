CCCWA COVID-19 Weekly Star #19

Immigration Asset Disposal — Guest Speaker Attorney Cai Qing

← Back to News
Announcement August 24, 2020

During the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, the Chinese Chamber of Commerce in Washington State (CCCWA) launched its "Weekly Star" (每周一星) online series — a recurring webinar bringing together professionals and experts to address the most pressing concerns facing the Chinese-American business community in the Greater Seattle area.

The 19th installment of the series featured Attorney Cai Qing of Cai Law Firm as a special guest speaker. Attorney Cai addressed the complex challenges that Chinese immigrants face when managing and disposing of assets across international borders — a topic that became especially urgent during the pandemic when in-person consultations, international travel, and cross-border financial operations were severely disrupted.

Key Topics Covered

Remote notarization: With physical access to notaries and embassies restricted, Attorney Cai explained the legal validity of remote notarization for documents involving US-based property and assets, and which countries and jurisdictions recognize these procedures.

Inheritance and estate transfer: For families with assets in both China and the US, Attorney Cai outlined the procedures for transferring inherited property across borders, including required documentation and timelines. He highlighted common pitfalls that can delay or invalidate inheritance claims.

Real estate disposal from abroad: Many Chinese immigrants who own US real estate found themselves unable to return to the US during the pandemic. Attorney Cai discussed how to legally authorize a local representative to manage or sell property on your behalf, including power-of-attorney requirements.

Cross-border fund transfers: With currency controls and pandemic-era banking restrictions in place, Attorney Cai explained legal channels for moving funds between China and the US, including IRS reporting requirements for foreign financial accounts (FBAR and FATCA compliance).

About the Weekly Star Series

CCCWA's Weekly Star series was launched in early 2020 in response to the pandemic, providing a free, accessible platform for Chinese-American business owners and community members to access expert guidance during an unprecedented time. Each session featured a different guest speaker covering a topic relevant to the community's immediate needs — from healthcare and finance to legal affairs and business strategy.

The series became one of CCCWA's most impactful community programs during the pandemic, reaching hundreds of members and non-members alike through live streaming and recorded broadcasts.
